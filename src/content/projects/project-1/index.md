Between 2017 and 2018, I designed, built, and operated a small cryptocurrency mining farm from the ground up, managing everything from hardware sourcing to financial tracking and eventual resale of the equipment.

The operation was split across four mining units:

- **Three custom GPU rigs** ("Simplet", "L3 Spirit", and a GTX-based rig), together running 24 GPUs (AMD Radeon RX 470/480 and Nvidia GTX 1070) for Ethereum mining.
- **One Antminer L3+ ASIC** dedicated to Litecoin mining.

Each rig was built from individually selected components — ASRock H81 Pro BTC motherboards (chosen for their multi-GPU riser support), Celeron/Pentium CPUs, Seasonic and Cooler Master platinum/gold PSUs sized for the real power draw of each GPU set, and PCIe USB risers to connect up to six GPUs per board. I sourced parts across multiple vendors in France, Germany, and Spain (Topachat, Hardware.fr, Amazon.de/.fr/.es, Smidistri, La Boutique du Mineur), comparing pricing, EAN references, and shipping costs to optimize the build budget.

Beyond the physical builds, I managed the full financial side of the project: tracking around €17,000 in total hardware investment across the rigs, ASIC, and shared infrastructure (electrical setup, shelving, shipping), and maintaining a detailed ledger to split costs and proceeds with a business partner. Once the operation wound down, I organized the resale of the hardware (GPUs, PSUs, components) through eBay and private sales, bringing the project close to breakeven despite the volatility of mining profitability and secondhand hardware prices.

This project gave me hands-on experience in hardware procurement and budgeting, small-scale infrastructure operations, and the practicalities of running a capital-intensive project with a partner — skills that carry directly into the operational and analytical rigor I bring to crypto support and account management roles today.
